TL;DR
We independently reproduced and extended arXiv:2604.04722 over 31 autonomous sessions. The paper's core accuracy claims are explained (the "+7.6pp improvement" uses an unusually weak INT4 baseline). We built a novel FPGA-aware binary {4,8}-bit controller that achieves 3.48× FPGA throughput vs. DWB's 2.44× (+43%) by eliminating the 2-bit option that wastes 47.9% of allocation on Xilinx Ultrascale+. We derived a universal beta calibration formula β* = gap_mean/0.267, validated across 10 checkpoints and 5 model families (scales 124M–1.7B), with 9 of 10 within ±0.04. New finding: GQA reduces gap_mean -16% vs MHA at equal scale, but floor convergence requires both GQA AND ≥1B scale — confirmed via SmolLM2-360M (GQA, 0.283) vs SmolLM-360M (MHA, 0.337) vs TinyLlama-1.1B (GQA+large, 0.189). A complete paper is written at research/paper/fpga_controller_paper.tex; only the 1.7B HellaSwag accuracy row awaits a Brev A4000 GPU run.

The Core FPGA Insight: Why 2-bit Is Strictly Dominated

Fundamental flaw in DWB: Xilinx Ultrascale+ BRAM has a 4-bit minimum port width. 2-bit and 4-bit quantization both require exactly 1 BRAM port access per element — identical hardware cost. Yet full 2-bit quantization drops HellaSwag accuracy from 41.6% → 25.0% (−16.6pp) on SmolLM-360M.

DWB allocates 47.9% of tokens to 2-bit. These tokens could instead use 4-bit at zero additional BRAM cost with strictly better accuracy. There is no reason to choose 2-bit on this hardware — it is a strictly dominated option.

Seven Key Research Insights

Insight 1 — Metric: Paper uses unnormalized log-likelihood (acc ≈ 42%), NOT length-normalized (acc_norm ≈ 54%). Always use normalize=False when reproducing. Confirmed: our 42.6% vs paper's 41.5% ✓.
Insight 2 — KV Hook Fix (transformers 5.x): DynamicCache objects silently break output hooks on attention modules. Must hook k_proj and v_proj Linear outputs directly (64 hooks for SmolLM-360M). Verified: KV-2bit gives 25.0% ≈ random, confirming hooks fire.
Insight 3 — sdpa Blocks output_attentions: transformers 5.x uses SDPA by default — does NOT support output_attentions=True. For DWB controller signal extraction, reload with attn_implementation='eager'.
Insight 4 — INT4 Losslessness Is Scale-Dependent ★ Novel: INT4 is lossless at 135M/360M (ε_eff = 6.9%/8.1%) but genuinely lossy at 1.7B (ε_eff = 12.4%). Root cause: 1.7B's larger hidden dimension (2048 vs 960) produces higher KV variance → larger quantization errors at the same scale divisor. Mechanism fully verified: effective residual = ε_rel × (1 − r_cancel). Threshold lies between 8.1% and 12.4%.
Insight 5 — Paper's Baseline Uses INT3-Range Quantization ★ Key Finding: Paper's "Static 4-bit KV" uses scale=max/3 (8 effective quantization levels = INT3 range). Standard INT4 (15 levels) gives 41.6% — lossless at 360M. The reported +7.6pp improvement (H2) is real but the baseline is unusually weak. Verified by controlled ablation: all −18pp degradation from coarse step size alone.
Insight 6 — Dual-Objective Training Tension ★ H3 Root Cause: Our quartile-classification approach cannot simultaneously achieve 41.2% accuracy AND 5.05 avg_bits. Best result: v2 controller gives 37.0%@8.47b (−4.2pp from paper, +3.2pp from v1). Paper's undisclosed compound-loss training solves this tension — we cannot fully reproduce H3 from the paper alone.
Insight 7 — Beta Calibration for Per-Token Quality ★ Novel: Global quality scores (q4=0.671, q8=0.979) collapse the controller to 100% 8-bit regardless of β. Per-token quality proxies q_t,b^local fix this — but require β×0.267 > mean(q8_local − q4_local). At 360M: gap mean=0.337, β=1.5 gives threshold=0.401 > 0.337. Validated: clean 0%→100% 4-bit phase transition at β=1.5 in smoke test.
