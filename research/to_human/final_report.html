H3 Deep Dive: Controller Sensitivity & Dual-Objective Tension

Two objectives, one undisclosed training procedure

The paper claims DWB achieves 41.2% (≈ FP16) at only 5.05 average bits. Our sensitivity study reveals this requires simultaneous optimization of both classification accuracy and bit-budget — which our quartile-labeling approach cannot do.

v1 Problem: Under-trained controller

val_acc=0.366 assigns too many tokens to 2-bit. Bit dist: {2: 47.9%, 16: 11.4%}. Controller degrades accuracy worse than standard INT4 at more bits (5.03 vs 4.0).

v2 Problem: Conservative "bimodal" assignment

val_acc=0.446 overcorrects. Bit dist: {2: 41.7%, 16: 41.8%} — bimodal split. Right accuracy range (~37-41%) but 1.68× too many bits (8.47 vs 5.05). No middle-ground assignments.

Diagnosis: The paper uses compound loss (α·CE + β·latency + γ·quality) to jointly optimize accuracy and compression. Our quartile-classification approach optimizes classification accuracy only, leading to either under-assignment (v1) or over-assignment (v2) of high bits.

Closing H3 fully requires: (1) end-to-end training with the paper's latency penalty, or (2) the paper's exact training corpus/hyperparameters — both undisclosed.

Key Findings

1Evaluation metric matters critically

Paper uses unnormalized acc (~42%), NOT acc_norm (~54%). lm-eval default is acc_norm — would give the wrong model entirely. Confirmed: FP16 unnorm = 42.6% vs paper's 41.5% (Δ=+1.1pp ✅).

2KV cache hooks silently fail with DynamicCache (transformers 5.x)

transformers 5.x uses DynamicCache objects — hooks on attention outputs find nothing to quantize. Fix: Hook k_proj and v_proj Linear submodules directly (64 hooks for SmolLM-360M). Verified: 2-bit → 25% (near-random), confirming hooks fire.

3sdpa attention blocks output_attentions

Default sdpa attention doesn't support output_attentions=True. Fix: Reload with attn_implementation='eager' for DWB signal extraction pass.

4INT4 losslessness is scale-dependent — mechanism fully verified ★★

Standard INT4 is lossless at 135M/360M (15 heads, eff_residual=8.1%) but lossy at 1.7B (32 heads, eff_residual=12.4%). Decision threshold: between 8.1% and 12.4%.

5Paper's INT4 baseline uses coarse step size (≈ 8 effective levels) ★★

int4_int3range (scale=max/3, 8 levels) = 33.0% — matches paper's 33.6% (Δ=-0.6pp). Standard INT4 (scale=max/7, 16 levels) = lossless (~42%). Controlled ablation confirms all degradation comes from coarse step size (scale=max/3 → -18pp), not range clipping (0pp additional).
